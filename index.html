Education and Career

In 1977, James A. Gosling received his B.Sc degree in Computer Science from the University of Calgary. Then in 1983, James finally obtained a Ph.D majoring in Computer Science from Carnegie Mellon University, with the dissertation title "The Algebraic Manipulation of Constraints".

When he finished his doctoral program, he wrote a version of emacs (gosmacs). Before he decided to join Sun Microsystems, James built a multi-processor version of Unix while still in college. Seeing these interests and talents, one time a friend of his parents took James A. Gosling to the computer laboratory at the University of Calgary to develop his talents and interests, at that time he was only 14 years old.

In 1984, James A. Gosling decided to join a small company that was just established at that time, namely Sun Microsystems. Initially James A. Gosling was involved in a development project called the Green Project, one of his works was a device called Star Seven, which was shaped like a PDA. Initially, the device was used as a home application sharing controller that could control television, answer the phone, and share other things. The tool can be used for various applications because it is based on a programming language that is not dependent on a particular processor. Along with the development of the name of the programming language which was originally called Oak, it was changed to Java until now.
